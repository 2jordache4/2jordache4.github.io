About Me


Hello! I received my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science from Pacific University in 2024. I'm now pursuing my Master's in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Boston University. I am most interested in exploring multi-agent systems applications in real or simulated environments.


Education


Pacific University

During my time at Pacific University, I was a member of the Women in Computer Science club and, at different times, both a member and team manager of the men's tennis team. I also worked at the university help desk for three years as a computer support technician. For my senior thesis, I conducted research on the Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) for Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM). Some notable courses I took include Multivariable Calculus, Linear Algebra, Mathematical Probability and Statistics, Introduction to Analysis, Introduction to Databases, and Data Structures.


Boston University

I am in my second semester at Boston University, where I have worked on both applied and simulated robotics projects. During my first year in the program, I have completed or am currently taking courses including Robot Motion Planning, Introduction to Robotics and Autonomous Systems, Soft Robotics, Learning from Data, Image and Video Processing, and Introduction to Embedded Systems.


Experience


I am currently a lead teacher at The Robo Hub, where I design and teach robotics and programming concepts to the youth of Cambridge! In this role, I explore sensor functionality, real-world application, and efficient design using robotics kits. Additionally, I help build a foundation of programming skills by applying loops, events, conditionals, and functions.

Outside of my current job, I have held many other positions such as: teaching assistant for a high school mechatronics program, a math and computer science tutor, robotics instructor, and computer support technician.
